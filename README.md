## What Is Ketamine and Its Medical Uses?
Ketamine is a dissociative anesthetic originally developed in the 1960s. In medical settings, it's used for:
- **Pain management**: Effective for chronic pain conditions unresponsive to other treatments.
- **Anesthesia**: Commonly administered in surgeries or emergency procedures.
- **Mental health treatment**: Low-dose ketamine therapy has shown promise for depression, PTSD, and anxiety, often via infusions or nasal sprays like esketamine (Spravato), approved by Health Canada.

Unlike recreational use, medical ketamine is strictly controlled. In Canada, it's a Schedule I drug, meaning it requires a prescription from a qualified physician.

- **Quality Issues**: Street or online illicit ketamine may be mixed with harmful additives.

For those seeking alternatives, explore clinical trials via ClinicalTrials.gov or Canadian research institutions.
